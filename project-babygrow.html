Babygrow

UX/UI Design//UI Development

When

Jan-May 2020

Role

  • UX Researcher
  • UI Designer
  • UI Developer

What

How might we sugest activities to a trusted caregiver that are safe and easy to make and that are tailored to baby's growth?

Why?

Our colleagues brought us awareness for the shortage of daycares.

They're often nor accessible or affordable to everyone, so more and more parents are leaving their kids with a trusted caregiver, whom might not have the knowledge or ability to create a thriving and safe environment for the kid's growth.

Problem Discovery

Lack of child-care instituitions

Lack of knowledge of the caregiver

What?

We believed we could build a product to help caregivers provide a safe education at home.

But what?

We did some research first

Competitive Analysis

Before we got too excited with this project and because the market for learning platforms can be a little saturated we analysed the competition and searched for painpoints and ways to improve our own concept.

photo

We tested the apps for a week and learned that in general the acitivties were very goal-based and that was not what we were aiming for.

How?

We needed to learn more about what the parents needs and how we could build a product for children. So we decided to do user interviews with both parents and chilcare experts.

User Interviews

8 parents

3 child educators

  • What's your opinion about an education at home?
  • What do you think of the age brackets in education until the first grade?
  • In your opinion, is there any stage in the life of child that's it's more favorable to interaction with other children?
  • What's the 3 main problems with formal education in daycare?
Design Sprint

To make a better use of the data we gathered, and to create a little more of collaboration between the team, we decided to do a design sprint.

We built user journeys, how might we's, and left the sprint with a rough prototype and a bunch of constraints to keep in mind.

photo

photo

User Journey

photo

How Might We?

How might we sugest activities to a trusted caregiver that are safe and easy to make and that are tailored to baby's growth?

Product Constraints

  • Activities had to be curated by chilcare professionals
  • The activities could not be defined by the child's age
  • We had to access the child's learning progress so we could tailor the activities
  • We had to use non-biased language, so the caregiver would give us valuable input
  • It had to be about the child, not the caregiver

Testing & Improvements

After some informal testing with the experts we started to iterate and do improvements of some key features of the platform

First improvement
Got rid of the chatbot

Upon first testing, we noticed some resitance from the part of the users when it came to the chatbot.

After some dicussion, we decided to transform the chatbot in a form so it would be more simple for the user.

photo

Second improvement
Changed the notes feature

When we tested the app with child experts, we noticed this feature had little guidance.

We changed the feature from an open commentary to a check list with some pre-filled examples so parents would be more compelled to anottate the child's progress there.

photo

Third improvement
Improved app copy

The child experts we worked with were worried about some of the terms and clarity of the app.

We improved the overall copy of the app so parents would not have so many bias towards the kid's progress.

photo

Final Solution

After some months of hard work and some build up in our marketing channels, our product was ready to launch.

Adapts to the child growth
  • Initial form to get a good grasp on the child learning process
  • Weekly forms to adapt the activities to the child capabilities
  • Intuitive UI and non-biased language

photo

Weekly Activities
  • New activities every week that are created by chilcare professionals
  • The activities provided are also safe and use everyday materials that everyone has on hand

photo

Design System

To craft the UI we did a design system on Figma.

Used a modular scale system for the typeface and a 8px grid.

Everything was built from scratch by our team, from the logo to illustrations.
